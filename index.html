India Gate

Located in New Delhi, India Gate is a majestic war memorial built in honor of the Indian soldiers who died in World War I. Standing 42 meters tall, it is a symbol of national pride and a popular tourist attraction, beautifully lit at night and surrounded by lush lawns perfect for evening strolls.

Red fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, the Red Fort is a grand Mughal fortress built in 1648 by Emperor Shah Jahan. Made of striking red sandstone, it once served as the royal residence. Today, it stands as a symbol of India's rich history and hosts the Independence Day celebrations each year.

Mysore palace

Located in Karnataka, Mysuru Palace is a magnificent royal residence of the Wadiyar dynasty. Known for its Indo-Saracenic architecture, intricate carvings, and grand Durbar Hall, it shines brightly when illuminated at night. The palace is a major attraction, especially during the vibrant Dussehra festival.

Cruise through the serene backwaters of Kerala on a traditional Alleppey houseboat. Surrounded by lush greenery, coconut palms, and village life, these floating homes offer a peaceful escape with modern comforts.
